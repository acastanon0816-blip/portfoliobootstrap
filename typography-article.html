Websites Compared

For this comparison article, I evaluated two snowboard shop retail websites, looking at their typography choices for readability, hierarchy, spacing, and overall professionalism.

Invasion Snowboard Shop (West Dover, VT)

Link: https://www.invasionvt.com/

The Invasion Snowboard Shop website employs a straightforward, functional type hierarchy with clear navigation labels and basic body text. Its headings appear in bold, simple sans-serif fonts that focus on readability over stylistic expression. The navigation menu and product listings are tightly spaced, which could make the user's ability to scan quickly slightly challenging but it is consistent across screens. Overall, it appears they preferred type that promotes clarity andusability, reflecting the practical nature of a local board shop site. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

Darkside Snowboards (Killington, VT)

Link: https://darksidesnowboards.com/

Darkside’s website is a more modern presentation with larger, sans-serif fonts that create a slightly more polished feel compared to Invasion. Its headings and category labels are heavier and more spaced, which makes it easier for the eye to navigate through the menu and product listings. The typography in headings contrasts more strongly with the body text, creating a clearer visual hierarchy. However, the smaller interface text may appear crowded to users given the multiple navigation elements, which could mildly impact the overall readability. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

Comparison and Conclusion

After reviewing both websites, it is clear that both use sans-serif typography to prioritize readability and consistency. They differ, however, in execution. Invasion’s typeface choices are functional and closely packed, reflecting its more utilitarian layout, while Darkside uses bolder, more modern font weights and spacing to create clearer separation between text elements. Darkside’s typography overall seems to improve hierarchy and ease of scanning, making it easier for users to identify navigation, categories, and calls-to-action at a glance. Invasion’s typography is more compact and uniform, which keeps text elements tight which could make it less easily scannable. For future improvement, both sites could increase font size contrasts between headings and body text to furtherimprove readability and emphasize hierarchy.
