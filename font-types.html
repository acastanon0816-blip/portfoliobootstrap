Different Types of Fonts

Fonts are often typically grouped by appearance and use. Choosing the right type of font can impact tone, readability, and professionalism.

Serif Fonts

Serif fonts are often seen as more traditional or academic. They work well for reading long texts. Sample: Times New Roman

Download example serif font: Merriweather (Google Fonts)

Sans Serif Fonts

Sans serif fonts usually feel modern and clean and are commonly used for websites. Sample: Arial

Download example sans serif font: Roboto (Google Fonts)

Display Fonts

Display fonts are designed for headings and branding, adding personality. They are not particularly well suited for long paragraphs. Sample: Impact

Download example display font: Oswald (Google Fonts)

Script Fonts

Script fonts give the allusion of handwriting making them feel a bit more elegant or casual. They are not intended for body text because they can be difficult to read. Sample: Brush Script MT

Download example script font: Pacifico (Google Fonts)

Tips for Selecting the Right Font

  • Use readable fonts for body text.
  • Use display or script fonts mostly for headings or logos.
  • Limit the number of fonts per page to keep designs consistent and clean.
